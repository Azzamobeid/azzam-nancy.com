Arriving in Venice

The major airport in Venice is Marco Polo (VCE), which is located on the mainland and not the island itself.  There are 3 different ways to travel to Venice from the airport. 

Alilaguna Waterbus

Public boat running regularly between the airport and Venice. 

  • Adult one-way fares range from 8 to 15 euros, depending on distance, with a reduction for round trips on some routes. "Time tickets" (24 or 72 hours) are also available.

  • You'll pay an extra euro if you buy your ticket on board, and you can save a euro or two by booking online.

  • The fare includes transportation of 1 suitcase and 1 hand luggage. Extra bags cost 3 euros each

Routes

  • Linea Blu or Blue Line (Airport-San Marco-Stazione Marittima), with intermediate stops at Murano Colonna, Fondamenta Nove, Ospedale, Bacini, Lido, Arsenale, San Zaccaria, San Marco, Zattere, and Giudecca (Hilton Molino Stucky) before reaching the Marittima cruise terminal.

  • Linea Arancio or Orange Line (Airport-Rialto-San Marco) with intermediate stops at Fondamenta Nove, Madonna Dell-Orto, Guglie, San Stae, Rialto, and Sant'Angelo.

  • Linea Rossa or Red Line (Airport-Lido-San Marco), a seasonal route with stops at Murano Museo and the Lido di Venezia on its way to San Marco. This line normally operates from the end of April until early November.

  • (The Alilaguna map also shows a "Linea Verde," or "Green Line," but this is a guided boat tour, not a transportation service.)

  • If all of the above seems confusing, just go to the Alilaguna ticket booth, show the location of your hotel on a map, and let the ticket clerk decide which boat you need to catch

Taxi

Land taxi to the Piazzale Roma ( Vanice car/bus parking) for €50 or a water taxi to your hotel for €110-120 

Mestre Airport Bus

Take a Mestre airport bus from VCE to the Mestre railroad station then the train to Venezia Santa Lucia Railroad Station

Transportation inside Venice

The main form of transportation other than walking is the waterbus or as called by locals the Vaporetto, the company is called ACTV. 

You can download the AVM Venezia app, which is a new app that allows you to buy tickets. 

See the link for fares and multi day passes: 

http://europeforvisitors.com/venice/articles/venice-vaporetto-and-bus-fare-tables.htm

Transportation options are also available as part of the Venice city pass. 

Venice City Pass

http://www.veneziaunica.it/en/e-commerce/services

Many options are available including Museums, Churches, transportation, parking, tours and even wifi access. 

Getting the Venice city pass will save you the long lines especially at the San Marco Cathedral.

Wireless Service

You can buy a temporary SIM card. There are a couple of major cellphone services. When we compared last year, we ended up buying from company called tre ( http://www.tre.it). 

Do not buy phone cards from the booths at the airport; they are way too expensive. 

Staying in Venice

There are many hotels available, which vary in their star rating and locations. There are also many people who rent their apartments to tourists for sometimes cheaper than a hotel. You can find some of these apartments on these two websites: 

www.vrbo.com

www.airbnb.com

More Information

Venice is a magical city and just walking around will give you plenty to see. Many day trips to neighboring cities such as Verona and Padua can be made by train. 

A great website which include very useful information to plan your trip:

http://europeforvisitors.com/venice/
